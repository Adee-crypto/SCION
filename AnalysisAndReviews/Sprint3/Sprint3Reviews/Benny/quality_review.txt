The use of a List<BlockList> with delegation methods like HasBlockAt and TryBreakBlockAt keeps the collision surface area narrow, meaning new block behaviors can be added to BlockList without touching the manager's core logic.

The ManageCollision method handles both X and Y axes but duplicates the index recalculation (leftIndex, rightIndex, topIndex, bottomIndex) for each axis. Extracting this into a small helper would reduce the risk of the two blocks drifting out of sync when changes are made.
